What I Did

End-to-end real estate analytics workflow.

I processed and analysed 18,085 residential property transactions recorded across Dubai from January to July 2026. The data-preparation process standardised transaction dates, property types, locations, transaction categories, prices, property sizes, and nearby-infrastructure attributes while preserving the original records for traceability.

I performed exploratory and statistical analysis to evaluate transaction distribution, price per square metre, total transaction value, property-type differences, geographic price concentration, monthly patterns, and the relationship between property size and value. Suspicious observations were identified through transparent data-quality flags rather than being automatically deleted.

For reporting, I prepared structured analytical outputs using Python and SQL, then developed a four-page Power BI dashboard covering market overview, price and area analysis, property-type performance, and monthly trends. The final interpretation translated the results into valuation and market-segmentation recommendations while distinguishing statistical association from direct causation.

Key Insights

What the analysis revealed.

Flats dominated transaction volume

Flats represented approximately 88.1% of all recorded transactions, making apartment-type properties the dominant market segment in the dataset.

Property type affected unit pricing

ANOVA indicated statistically significant differences in price per square metre across property types (F = 427.87, p < 0.001).

Bluewaters led the qualified area ranking

After applying a minimum threshold of 20 transactions, Bluewaters recorded the highest average price at approximately AED 57.6K per square metre.

Size explained total value more than unit rate

Property size had a weak relationship with price per square metre (r = 0.114) but a strong relationship with total transaction value (r = 0.753).

Recorded infrastructure access aligned with premiums

Properties with recorded nearby metro stations or malls showed an average price premium of approximately 34%, although this association does not establish causation.

Standard sales were the dominant transaction type

Standard sales accounted for approximately 83.4% of the recorded transactions, indicating a strong concentration in the main sales category.

Business Recommendations

Recommended actions from the analysis.

Prioritise location and property type

Use geographic area and property type as primary valuation variables because they explain unit-price differences more clearly than property size alone.

Apply minimum sample thresholds

Rank areas only after applying a minimum transaction count and display the sample size to prevent decisions based on isolated high-value observations.

Use median prices for skewed markets

Prioritise median price indicators alongside averages because luxury transactions create strongly right-skewed price and value distributions.

Separate suspicious records before aggregation

Keep flagged records traceable, but exclude or analyse them separately when calculating market benchmarks and area-level averages.

Treat infrastructure as a market indicator

Use metro and mall access as indicators of established locations, but avoid claiming a direct causal premium without additional geospatial controls.

Expand the valuation model

Add bedroom count, building age, completion status, exact coordinates, developer, and project-level attributes to improve future pricing and investment analysis.
