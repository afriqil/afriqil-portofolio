What I Did

End-to-end analytics workflow.

I processed and audited more than 11.15 million calendar records together with 30,259 Airbnb listings across New York City. The workflow covered schema inspection, missing-value review, duplicate checks, technical sentinel validation, format standardization, and non-destructive cleaning of pricing, availability, review, host, and location fields.

I integrated listing, calendar, and neighbourhood reference data, resolved snapshot inconsistencies, and engineered indicators for market activity, estimated occupancy, estimated annual revenue, room type, capacity, and host profile. Exploratory and statistical analyses were then used to compare performance across boroughs, neighbourhoods, listing segments, and host characteristics.

For dashboard development, I created a fact-dimension star schema, exported validated Power BI-ready tables, and built DAX measures for supply, pricing, estimated occupancy, estimated revenue, ratings, and host performance. The final four-page dashboard communicates executive performance, geographic patterns, pricing and revenue, and host-level insights.

Key Insights

What the analysis revealed.

Manhattan market leadership

Manhattan had the largest market supply, the highest median nightly price at approximately USD 240.56, and the highest median estimated annual revenue among active listings at USD 26,216.

Brooklyn activity performance

Brooklyn recorded the highest positive-activity rate at 46.65% and the highest median estimated occupancy at 49.32% among active listings.

Superhost performance gap

Superhost listings achieved a 73.98% positive-activity rate compared with 32.08% for non-Superhosts, alongside higher median estimated annual revenue.

Hotel-room price-demand imbalance

Hotel rooms commanded the highest median nightly price at approximately USD 447 but showed considerably lower estimated occupancy, indicating a potential pricing imbalance.

Estimated metrics require caution

Calendar-derived occupancy and revenue are analytical estimates rather than confirmed booking or payment records, so the results should be interpreted as directional evidence.

Business Recommendations

Recommended actions from the analysis.

Segment pricing strategy

Develop pricing benchmarks by borough, neighbourhood, and room type instead of applying one market-wide pricing rule.

Replicate high-performing host practices

Investigate and replicate the operational practices of high-performing Superhosts, including listing quality, communication, availability management, and guest experience.

Prioritize low-activity listings

Use dashboard filters to identify low-activity listings and evaluate price, room type, location, capacity, and host characteristics together.

Reassess premium hotel pricing

Test narrower pricing ranges for premium hotel-room listings where high nightly rates coincide with weak estimated occupancy.

Monitor pricing experiments

Track activity, estimated occupancy, estimated revenue, and price changes before scaling any pricing or listing intervention.
