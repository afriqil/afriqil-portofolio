What I Did

End-to-end pricing analytics workflow.

I analysed 80,000 Apple product-price observations recorded across Amazon and Flipkart between 2020 and 2026. The data-preparation process covered product-name standardisation, category and storage normalisation, price and discount validation, condition and stock review, platform consistency checks, and careful assessment of extreme premium-price and review-count observations.

I performed exploratory analysis to compare pricing, discounts, ratings, stock availability, product condition, storage capacity, product category, and platform behaviour. Statistical testing was then used to evaluate price differences across product segments, measure the relationship between discount and customer rating, and compare discounts during official sale events with non-sale periods.

For reporting, I prepared analytical outputs in Python and Google BigQuery, then developed a three-page Power BI dashboard covering executive performance, pricing analysis, and customer insights. The final interpretation translated statistical results into targeted pricing and promotion recommendations while clearly separating statistical significance from confirmed commercial impact.

Key Insights

What the analysis revealed.

Discount intensity increased

Average discount increased from approximately 8.7% in 2020 to 26.6% in 2026, indicating greater reliance on promotional pricing over time.

Sale events produced deeper discounts

Official sale-event observations averaged approximately 33.7% discount compared with 20.3% outside sale periods, and the difference was statistically significant.

Mac retained premium positioning

Mac recorded the highest average selling price at approximately USD 1,382.49, reinforcing its position as the premium category in the dataset.

Apple Watch received the largest markdowns

Apple Watch recorded the highest average discount at approximately 38.64%, suggesting more aggressive promotional treatment than other categories.

Discount was not a quality signal

The relationship between discount and customer rating was weak, with a correlation of approximately -0.31, so larger discounts did not indicate higher customer satisfaction.

Platform pricing was broadly similar

Amazon and Flipkart showed nearly identical pricing strategies, suggesting limited platform-level differentiation in observed Apple product prices.

Business Recommendations

Recommended actions from the analysis.

Segment discount policies

Set discount ranges by product category, storage capacity, condition, stock position, and promotion period rather than applying uniform markdowns.

Protect Mac premium positioning

Maintain disciplined pricing for Mac products unless inventory pressure or a clearly defined campaign objective justifies a deeper discount.

Re-evaluate Apple Watch markdowns

Review whether the category’s aggressive average discount is necessary for turnover or whether narrower markdowns could protect profitability.

Use sale events selectively

Concentrate deeper discounts on time-sensitive campaigns, inventory reduction, and products with demonstrated stock pressure rather than all products.

Add commercial outcome data

Evaluate future promotion performance using sales volume, conversion, revenue, gross margin, and inventory days before automating discount decisions.

Avoid unnecessary platform price competition

Maintain competitive pricing across Amazon and Flipkart while avoiding platform-level markdowns that do not produce measurable commercial benefits.
